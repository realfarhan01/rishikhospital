What is Rhinoplasty?

Rhinoplasty, commonly known as nose surgery or a nose job, is a surgical procedure aimed at altering the shape or structure of the nose. It can be performed for cosmetic reasons, such as reducing the size of the nose or changing the angle, or for functional reasons, such as improving breathing difficulties caused by a deviated septum.

Who is an Ideal Candidate for Rhinoplasty?

Ideal candidates for rhinoplasty at Rishik Hospitals are individuals who:

Are in good overall health.

Have realistic expectations about the procedure and its outcomes.

Want to enhance their appearance or correct functional issues related to the nose.

Are at least 18 years old (as the nose continues to grow until this age).

Types of Rhinoplasty Procedures We Offer

Cosmetic Rhinoplasty: Reshapes the nose to improve its appearance, such as reducing a hump, narrowing the nostrils, or correcting asymmetry.

Functional Rhinoplasty: Corrects breathing problems caused by structural issues like a deviated septum or nasal obstruction.

Revision Rhinoplasty : For patients who are dissatisfied with the results of a previous nose surgery.

Non-Surgical Rhinoplasty : A non-invasive option for small aesthetic changes using dermal fillers.

The Rhinoplasty Procedure at Rishik Hospitals

The rhinoplasty procedure involves:

Anesthesia: The surgery is performed under local or general anesthesia, depending on the complexity of the procedure.

Surgical Approach: Our surgeons may opt for an open or closed approach, depending on the extent of the changes required. The incision is usually made inside the nose, but for more complex cases, a small incision may be made across the columella (the area between the nostrils).

Nose Reshaping: The surgeon will reshape the bone and cartilage as needed to achieve the desired outcome.

Recovery: After surgery, you will be monitored in our recovery room. You will receive detailed post-operative instructions to ensure a smooth healing process.

Recovery After Rhinoplasty Surgery

After rhinoplasty surgery, you can expect some swelling, bruising, and mild discomfort, which is normal. Most of the swelling will subside within a few weeks, but it may take several months for the final shape of the nose to fully settle. We will guide you through the recovery process and provide ongoing support to help you achieve the best possible results.
